Aluminium Scrap: HSN code Guide

Different grades of scrap (e.g., castings, turnings, or borings) may be tracked as separate inventory items, but they typically share the same HSN family (7602) because they are all intended for metal recovery or recycling. Using the correct HSN code is critical to avoid penalties during audits and ensure accurate data in GSTR-1 filings



As of 2025, the GST rate for all categories of aluminum waste and scrap in India is standardized at 18%. This applies to various forms of industrial discards, used products, and secondary materials categorized under Chapter 76 of the Harmonized System of Nomenclature (HSN).

HSN Code Item Categorization & Specifics Tax Bracket (GST)
7602.00.10 Aluminum Recyclables: Specialized scrap including pristine lithographic sheets, unsoiled low-copper clippings, and assorted solids (e.g., ISRI codes Tablet, Tabloid, Taboo, Taint). 18%
7602.00.11 Industrial By-products: Turnings, shavings, chips, milling waste, and filings; includes waste from coated or bonded sheets and foil under 0.2mm. 18%
7602.00.19 Factory Rejects: Other industrial discards and secondary production rejects not classified in specific sub-headings. 18%
7602.00.90 Residual Salvage: Broad category for "Other" aluminum waste and scrap materials not elsewhere specified. 18%
7601.20.91 Secondary Ingot/Liquid: Unwrought aluminum alloys in liquid or ingot form derived specifically from remelted waste and scrap. 18%
7603.20.00 Processed Powder/Flakes: Aluminum powders of lamellar structure and metallic flakes obtained from secondary processing. 18%


In the UK, HSN codes are referred to as Commodity Codes. While they share the same first six digits as the global Harmonized System (HS), the UK uses a 10-digit structure for imports and an 8-digit structure for exports to classify goods more specifically


UK applies a 20% Standard Rate VAT.
